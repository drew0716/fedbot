Evaluating for Impairments Whenever major events or changes in operating circumstances indicate that the carrying amount of an asset may not be recoverable, perform an evaluation of the recorded carrying value of the associated asset to determine if a write-down due to impairment is needed. 14 The following tests should be applied when such events or changes in circumstances occur or on an annual basis in the absence of such events or changes to determine if asset impairment is appropriate: A significant decrease in the fair value of an asset held for disposal. 15 A significant change in the extent or manner in which an asset is used or a significant physical change in an asset. A significant adverse change in legal factors or in the business climate that could affect the value of an asset or an adverse action or assessment by a regulator. An accumulation of costs significantly in excess of the amount originally expected to acquire or construct an asset where these costs are not anticipated to be recoverable in the future.
