Collins President and Chief Executive Officer Federal Reserve Bank of Boston Susan M. Collins took office as president and chief executive officer of the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston on July 1, 2022. Prior to her appointment, Dr. Collins was provost and executive vice president for academic affairs at the University of Michigan, as well as the Edward M. Gramlich Collegiate Professor of Public Policy and Professor of Economics. (She retains the latter designation at the university, on unpaid leave.) Dr. Collins also served for a decade as the Joan and Sanford Weill Dean of the university's Gerald R. Ford School of Public Policy. Previously, Dr. Collins was a professor of economics at Georgetown University, a senior fellow at the Brookings Institution, and a senior staff economist at the President's Council of Economic Advisers.
