Q18: What are "agency securities"? Updated: 2/5/2024 A18: The FOMC Policy on Investment and Trading for Committee Participants and Federal Reserve System Staff defines an agency security as an obligation that is a direct obligation of, or is fully guaranteed as to principal and interest by, any agency of the United States, including the Federal National Mortgage Association (Fannie Mae), Government National Mortgage Association (Ginnie Mae), and Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation (Freddie Mac). Agency securities include agency debt as well as agency mortgage-backed securities. Q19: What types of REITs are prohibited under the Policy for Covered Individuals? Conversely, equity REITs that concentrate in the ownership of real estate and pay out dividends based on the REITâ€™s rental income are permissible investments under the policy.
