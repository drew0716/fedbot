Loans made directly by the Banks to eligible borrowers and by SPVs to borrowers may be collateralized by pledged assets. These facilities include the following: Primary, secondary, seasonal, and other credit Main Street Expanded Loan Facility (MSELF) Main Street New Loan Facility (MSNLF) Main Street Priority Loan Facility (MSPLF) Municipal Liquidity Facility (MLF) Nonprofit Organization Expanded Loan Facility (NOELF) Nonprofit Organization New Loan Facility (NONLF) Paycheck Protection Program Lending Facility (PPPLF) Term Asset-Backed Securities Loan Facility (TALF) Bank Term Funding Program (BTFP) Loans extended by the Reserve Banks to consolidated limited liability companies (LLCs) are not considered in the following paragraphs because the loans are eliminated in consolidation. In addition, loans extended to consolidated LLCs that are recorded at fair value do not require an allowance for credit loss. FASB ASC Topic 326-20 establishes the general expectation of the recognition of lifetime expected allowance for credit losses on financial assets measured at amortized cost, commonly referred to as the Current Expected Credit Loss (CECL) model, regardless of whether the risk of credit loss is deemed to be remote.
