The Treasury, using the Exchange Stabilization Fund, committed $10 billion as credit protection to the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston (FRBB) and advanced $1.5 billion. Per the Credit Support Agreement with the Treasury, the FRBB agreed to pay to the Treasury a facility fee in respect of each MMLF loan. On April 12, 2021, the MMLF returned the credit protection of $1.5 billion and paid facility fees of $134 million to the Treasury. The PPPLF program extends credit to eligible financial institutions that originate Paycheck Protection Program loans, taking the loans as collateral at face value.
