The FOMC makes all decisions regarding the appropriate position or "stance" of monetary policy to help move the economy toward the congressionally mandated goals of maximum employment and price stability. The FOMC's 12 voting members include the 7 members of the Board of Governors; the president of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York; and 4 of the remaining 11 Reserve Bank presidents, who serve one-year terms on a rotating basis. In addition, all the remaining 7 Reserve Bank presidents attend FOMC meetings and participate in FOMC deliberations. The Board of Governors, the Federal Reserve Banks, and the Federal Open Market Committee work together to promote the health of the U.S. economy and the stability of the U.S. financial system. Tuned in to Economic Conditions through Data Collection and Analysis Teams of economists, analysts, examiners, and other professionals from across the Federal Reserve System continuously gather and analyze data and information from around the U.S. and abroad.
