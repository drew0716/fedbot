Overview: Federal Reserve System Boards of Directors Current Bank and Branch Directors Sectors Represented by Reserve Bank Directors Sectors Represented by Branch Directors Roles and Responsibilities of Directors Policies Governing Directors Overview: Federal Reserve System Boards of Directors As the central bank of the United States, the Federal Reserve System conducts the nation's monetary policy and helps to maintain a stable financial system. Three key components of the Federal Reserve System—the Federal Reserve Board of Governors (Board of Governors), the Federal Reserve Banks (Reserve Banks), and the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC)—interact to accomplish these goals. Pursuant to the Federal Reserve Act, each of the 12 Reserve Banks is subject to the supervision of a nine-member board of directors. Structure and Composition Reserve Bank Boards The Federal Reserve Act provides that Reserve Bank directors are divided into three classes—Class C, Class B, and Class A—of three directors each.
